How TPPA works: point the telescope at any patch of sky with a few stars (Dec ~60°+ recommended), enable tracking, then click Start. Polaris will slew the mount in RA by two times and plate-solve at each position. The three solved points constrain the mount's mechanical polar axis, and the difference vs. the true celestial pole gives your alignment error.

After TPPA, click Refine to enter a live capture/solve loop — adjust your tripod knobs and watch the error vector shrink in real time.

Calibrate light frames
For each selected light, the service applies (light − dark) / normalised_flat and writes a CALSTAT header recording which corrections ran. Auto-match picks the master with the same gain (and closest exposure for darks, same filter for flats).

Integrate frames into master light
Detects stars in each frame, picks the frame with the most stars as the alignment reference, fits an affine transform for every other frame, resamples, and reduces with the chosen method. Frames that fail to align are skipped (reported as dropped). Output goes to {rig}/integrated/{target}/{filter}/.
